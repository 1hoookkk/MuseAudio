## Why THIS Aesthetic?

### Low-FPS stutter (10 FPS mouth, "broken radio" text)
- Suggests something **struggling to manifest across a barrier**
- Reflects the discrete, quantized nature of digital signal processing
- Evokes **old séance photography** — slow shutter speeds capturing spirits mid-materialization

### Monochrome brutalism (deep black OLED, mint green phosphor)
- Looks like a **broken transmission device from 1975**
- Suggests this is hardware that *shouldn't work anymore* but still does
- Says: "Obviously haunted. Obviously unstable. Use at your own risk."

### Halftone mouth as scrying window
- Direct visualization of Z-plane pole formations
- You are literally watching the mathematical entity's structure shift as you morph
- A window into the complex plane

### Séance metaphor
- You sit before a black mirror (the OLED screen)
- You adjust ritual parameters (PAIR, MORPH, INTENSITY)
- A presence materializes (the mouth forms from pole constellations)
- It speaks cryptically ("Copper..." "Breathing..." "There.")
- You hope it doesn't **meltdown** (poles ≥ 0.93, NaN detected, fourth wall break)

## The Obsession

175 commits. 5 branches. 64,000 lines. Seven UI rebuilds.

We rebuilt the UI searching for the right visual language because **we needed an interface that looked like it could channel something from inside the machine**.

Generic knobs and meters? No. Those suggest **control**.

We needed an interface that suggests **communion**. **Invocation**. **Risk**.

The OLED aesthetic — monochrome, stuttering, brutal — is the only visual language that says:
> "There is something alive in here. It is made of mathematics. It is beautiful and unstable. When you turn these knobs, you are navigating the edge of chaos. Pay attention to the mouth. Pay attention to my words. If I say 'wtf (╯°□°)...', **back off intensity**."

## Design Implications

### UI Must Suggest
- **Communion** not control
- **Invocation** not operation
- **Risk** not safety
- **Portal** not panel

### Every Element Must Serve The Ritual
- Scrying mirror (main display)
- Manifestation window (mouth)
- Invocation controls (knobs)
- Transmission feed (status/utterances)

- OLED deep black (void/portal/scrying mirror)
- Phosphor green/amber (old CRT transmission)
- Halftone mouth (Z-plane geometry window)
